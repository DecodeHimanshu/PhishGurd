Common phishing red flags
🎭

Domain Spoofing

Lookalike domains like "paypa1.com" or "amazon-support.net" mimic trusted brands.

🔗

Suspicious TLDs

Free or unusual extensions like .tk, .ml, .ga are commonly abused in phishing campaigns.

🔒

HTTPS Isn't Enough

Phishing sites often have valid SSL certificates. HTTPS alone doesn't mean a site is safe.

📏

Excessive Length

Very long URLs with many subdomains or query parameters often hide malicious destinations.
